Automated Picking System

Situation

  • Logistics warehouses rely on manual labour for order picking.
  • There are disadvantages towards manual labour.
    • Human error.
    • High labour costs.
    • Inability to scale operations efficiently.
    • Risks of accidents and injuries.
  • Challenge: Prototype an automated solution to increase order picking rates.

Task

  • Elicit stakeholder needs and requirements for the prototyping process.
  • Transform stakeholder requirements into design output specifications.
  • Integrate identified subsystems into prototype solution.
  • Design the logic flow for the prototype solution.
  • Demonstrate the feasibility of the prototype solution.

Action

  • Conducted interviews with stakeholders to identify needs and requirements.
  • Created CAD drawings based on stakeholder requirements to identify necessary subsystems to realise prototype solution.
  • Assembled custom mounts for depth camera and suction cup for the TM5-700 collaborative arm.
  • Designed the logic flow for the prototype solution using flowcharts.
  • Conducted demonstrations on the TM5-700 under specific operational conditions to showcase prototype solution.

Result

  • Met stakeholder expectations for order picking rates.
  • Met stakeholder expectations for picking products that can be found in logistics warehouses.
