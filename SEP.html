Binder Jetter

Products are designed with the intention of users being able to see. However, approximately 1.1 billion people are visually impaired. Furthermore, 9 out of 10 visually impaired individuals report that medication or food label information is difficult to read. To address this issue, our team have conceptualized the Binder Jetter, a device that can extrude Braille onto any surface.

Starting with a food printing system, the team performed exploratory research to understand the functionalities of the food printing system. The team then conducted research to identify users that would benefit from the capabilities of a food printing system. Landing on visually impaired individuals, the team identified the pain points of visually impaired individuals in the food industry. The team then brainstormed potential solutions to address the pain points of visually impaired individuals in the food industry.

Braille embossers and NaviLens were identified as potential solutions to address the pain points of visually impaired individuals in the food industry. Comparisons were made between Braille embossers and NaviLens to determine the most suitable existing solution, with Braille embossers being selected due to visually impaired people being more familiar with Braille and NaviLens having issues due to environment constraints, such as lighting, noisiness, etc. Functionalities of Braille embossers and food printing systems were merged to identify the functionalities of the Binder Jetter.

The team discovered that 2D food printing systems, such as inkjet printers, are unable to perform the functionality of extruding Braille onto any surface, due to the lack of Z-axis movement. Binder jetting technology with concrete powder was chosen as the most suitable technology to elevate the surface due to needing only water to bind the powder together.
