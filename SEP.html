Braille Food Printer

Situation

  • Approximately 1.1 billion people worldwide are visually impaired.
  • Reading medication or food labels is challenging for visually impaired individuals.
  • Products are designed primarily for users with sight.
  • Challenge: Prototype a food printing system that elevates the surface of the product, allowing Braille to convey product information.

Task

    Concept Stage

  • Research on food printing systems.
  • Research on existing solultions for visually impaired people.
  • Identify missing functionalities of food printing systems from braille embossers.
  • Creation of high-level preliminary concepts that fulfils the functionalities of the system.
  • Analytical research to verify feasibility of preliminary concepts.

Action

  • Application of the Mission Analysis Process from the Systems Engineering Handbook to guide the team's direction in the concept stage.
  • Brainstorm on mechanisms of the solution.
  • Read research papers to analyse feasibility of the solution, such as tensile strength and curing time.

Result

  • Identified functionalities of the system.
  • Digital sketch of the prototype to be built.
