Edible Clear Coat Machine

Situation

  • Food labels are commonly used to convey food information to customers, raise brand awareness, and perform inventory management.
  • The problem is food labels are created for single-use, with the most common examples being on fresh produce in the form of stickers.
  • Customers would have to peel the food label off before consumption, and the food label quickly ends up in the bin or the environment.
  • These disposable food labels have been shown to have adverse effects on the environment.
  • Challenge: Given a 2D food printing system (Evebot), prototype a solution for sustainable food labelling.

Concept - Exploratory Research

    Objectives

    • Identify core functionalities needed for the prototype.
      1. Identify functionalities of an inkjet-based food printing system.
      2. Identify functionalities of a Laser Engraver (existing solution).
      3. Match and determine the functionalities of the prototype using semantics.
    • Explore preliminary concepts to assess technological risks and readiness level.
      1. Sketch a visual representation of the team's idea to attain common understanding and identify potential issues, such as technological risks.
      2. Design a concept map to understand the relationships between the different subsystems and the Evebot.

    Personal Contributions

    • Explored the domain of food printing systems and the problem space to study new ideas and define the prototype's functionality.
    • Concept Map: Utilized LucidChart to define subsystems and enablers as shown in the sketch and functionality table and defined meaningful relationships.

Concept - Selection and Refinement

    Objectives

    • Clarify and document conceptual operation of the prototype.
    • Design engineering models to verify feasibility.
    • Design an algorithm to accomplish the sequence diagram and implement in programming language.

    Personal Contributions

    • Activity Diagram: Designed a sequence diagram in UML to define how the prototype will be used.
    • Fritzing: Designed an electrical schematic to determine power requirements and hardware connections of each electrical component.
    • Programming: Created an Arduino Sketch in C++ to control the movement of the extruder platform, engage and disengage the locking mechanism, and change the LED and LCD to reflect the current state of the system.

Prototype - Demonstration and Verification

    Objectives

    • Demonstrate that the prototype solves the problem statement.
    • Demonstrate that the prototype meets the functionalities as determined in the concept stage.

Project Outcome

  • Met all core functionalities as defined in the verification tables. However, the prototype is unable to fully coat the product label on the banana peel.
  • Future work:
    • Partial coating on product label: Increase the storage capacity of the edible wax to ensure that the product label is fully coated on the banana peel.
